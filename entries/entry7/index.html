Smooth Tech and Gumbo

post output

Dear Parents,

Ever heard of Smooth Technology? I definitely didn’t before this week. They are a team of four? people that combine art and engineer to accomplish crazy feats. Their first emerging project was the iconic neon lights for Taylor Swift’s 1989 World Tour. That project really put them on the map and have since worked with big names like Katy Perry, Nicki Minaj, Billy Porter, and more. We were all blown away by their extensive organizational system since as engineers, they have to work with physical pieces to create prototypes in short timespans. They also shared how sometimes they don’t make that big of a profit since there are a lot of unfactored costs that come with big projects. They try to never turn down a company to avoid losing that contact.

We also visited Gumbo Collective, a communal working space for game designers. They invited us into their space and we were able to sit in their lounge area, a very cozy place with deep couches, warm baths of light, and games everywhere. We learned the power of community and how developers were able to find a community in an otherwise isolated job.
