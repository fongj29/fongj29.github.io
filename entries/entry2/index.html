Governor's Island

post output

Friday, September 15th, was the first day of my new class, NYC Design Safari. I had no idea what to expect, but I got an email that we would be going to our first field trip. We met up in class and took the subway down to Battery Park then walked 2 minutes to the ferry. Governor's island is south of Manhattan, past the Statue of Liberty. Governors Island is open to the public and serves as a public park. It offers a wide range of recreational activities, cultural events, and educational programs. We were most focused on the artists in residence which apparently means that artists are sponsored by certain organizations to stay at these houses and create art.

The second house I went into was part of the TAAC (Taiwanese American Arts Council). As I was looking at one of the pieces with my classmate, another observer told us that the vinyl artist, Eun Young Choi, was this amazing woman in the kitchen. She actually came up to me as I was looking closer. I noticed there were words nestled in the piece and I asked her what it meant. She talked about how she wanted to show the hidden history of Asian Americans since there are many notable events that are not talked about enough. She explained how the Gentlemen's Agreement of 1908 allowed the first Asian families to live on sugarcane plantations.. this sounded familiar to me and I asked if that was in Hawaii. It was, and I shared how I was from Hawaii and she was pleasantly surprised. She showed me the names of the last Native Hawaiian monarch, Queen Lili'uokalani, perched at the top of the piece.

I talked with the artist for what felt like 30 minutes as my friend just stood there. She is from Toronto so I could see her getting bored. We walked out and she wanted to go back to the dorm to study before she had a family dinner. After she left, I was left to wander the island by myself.
