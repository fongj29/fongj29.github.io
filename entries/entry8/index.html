New Blood Fashion

post output

Dear Parents,

I went to my first fashion show this week! My roommate, Kiki, had texted me on Monday about a fashion show that her friend was putting on. I didn’t look too into it but I agreed to go with her since it was a Friday night and I had nothing else planned. She later told me that the event was red black and white themed so we each looked for outfits the day before lol. I ended up getting a red top from a thrift store and she wore a black dress. It was fun getting ready together since she offered to do my hair and had this hair curling thing that could curl my hair for me.

We honestly got to the fashion show kind of late since doors opened at 8, but since it said it started at 8:30, Kiki suggested we leave at 8:10 since it was only a few minutes away. We went up a ritchety elevator and when we arrived on the floor, there was only a sign for an art studio. We opened the door and it was the backstage - all the models were getting ready to go out on the runway; the designers were rushing around and doing the last touchups. It was like a stereotypical nightmare. Kiki luckily spotted her friend who was running the thing and she rushed us through the curtain and to the actual runway area. There was a good chunk of people, mainly Chinese, a few that Kiki recognized.

The show started shortly after and I took my camera out... the lights went out and I was trying to autofocus. I didn’t realize that by doing this in the dark, my camera shoots a dull white light which was pretty obvious since it was pitch black. Rookie mistake. But the curtain also dropped right before the models were supposed to walk through sooo.

I had a good angle of the models but there was this guy right in front of me taking up the most space possible just to film the thing on his phone. But I looked at his screen throughout the show, and after the first 5 minutes, the video was getting crooked because he wasn’t paying attention. I was only going to take a few pics but the people next to me were irritating me with their plastic disposable cams and a whole plastic polaroid. Good stuff. I think the better photographers were in the middle under the main light because i saw their big lens. I wonder how their photos turned out. Kiki was next to me so I was showing her some of my photos throughout the show and she was hyping them up so that probably also gave me a confident boost.

It was an overall great time and I’m glad I got to go and get crazy shadows without having to use and figure out external flash and stuff.
